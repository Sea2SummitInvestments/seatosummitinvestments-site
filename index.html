Understanding Risk and Return in Modern Investing

Investing is ultimately a tradeoff between risk and reward. Over time, asset classes have delivered very different outcomes based not just on returns, but on durability of capital, cash flow, and tax treatment. Real asset–backed strategies, particularly multifamily real estate, have historically offered a compelling risk-adjusted tradeoff.

How to use this framework

  • Understand tax treatment early. Ordinary income vs. capital gains changes the real return.
  • Stress test the downside. Ask: “What breaks if rents fall, rates rise, or timelines slip?”
  • Prioritize durable investments. When two return profiles are similar, choose the investment backed by a durable asset.
  • Match liquidity to need. Don’t lock up capital you may need in the next 12–24 months.
